Long time, no see

June 8, 2024

Hey folks,

I’ve decided to reboot this blog from six years ago. A lot has changed; some has stayed the same. I’m going to write a few posts about some solo performance work that I’ve created over the past two years.


Photo credit: Ricardo Adame

Since my last post in 2018, I’ve finished my PhD and started a career as a UX designer-strategist. Given that I’ve ended my formal relationships with academic institutions and their resources, the focus of my work has shifted to do more with less. With a few exceptions, I don’t currently “write” much music for ensembles, but I’m more active with dancers and improvisers (sound-makers and movers). This work is less score-based and more relationship-based, and it’s also forced me to turn inward and examine my own personal artistic practices with regards to what I bring into these relationships. 

I’ve established myself in my hometown of Chicago as a collaborator on viola (acoustic and amplified), electronics, and motion-audio-triggered visuals, but I haven’t been initiating my own projects as much. I was happy to offer my talents to folks in the community doing their own projects, but honestly, I was tired of doing the administrative work associated with driving one’s own group projects. Anyways, there came a time where I wanted to bring all of my artistic practices together in an intimate solo space that I could control. 

What was I able to do as a self-contained solo performer? What ideas would I work with? What do I have to say or ask on stage? More soon.
