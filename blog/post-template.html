This is the opening paragraph. Start strong — a scene, a question, or a provocation. Draw the reader in before you begin to develop your argument or reflection. The first sentences are the most important.

Continue the essay here. Keep paragraphs to three to five sentences where possible. White space is your friend in long-form reading on screen. Don't be afraid to let an idea breathe before you move on to the next one.

A Subheading

Use subheadings sparingly — only when you're genuinely shifting to a new section of the essay, not simply moving to the next paragraph. Overuse of headers fragments the reading experience.

This paragraph continues the thought from the subheading. Notice how the body text is kept at a comfortable 16px with generous line-height — designed for sustained reading rather than quick scanning.

Another Section

Introduce the next section of your argument. Give concrete examples where possible — specificity in essayistic writing makes the abstract tangible and the general personal.
