2025 Reflections

January 11, 2026

2025 was wild. As someone who made the decision in 2018 to not become a “professional artist or academic”, I never imagined that I'd have 30 events in one year. I loved being busy, but it was stressful.

My projects this past year led me to develop new sides of my artistry. 2025 was the first time I created audio-reactive visuals for a live music performance, contributed viola tracks to someone’s solo album, made a string quartet record, curated an interdisciplinary concert series, created a camera-based installation at a gala, and started an video interviewing project.

a few highlights:

  • My first “notated” piece in many years was commissioned and performed by Cacie Miller, Dan Galat, and Kelly Quesada with assistance from Tuli Bera

  • Created a trio with Cristal Sabbagh and olula negre at the Chicago Jazz String Summit, at the invitation of Tomeka Reid (link)

  • Curated the Comfort Music+ series at Comfort Station

  • Recorded an album of improvised string quartet music with Dan Galat, Kelly Quesada, and Ben Willis

  • My partner E moved in with me <3

  • Performed topless (link)

  • Created a duet with Tuli Bera where we danced, made 16-channel electro-acoustic music, and created live projections. This was my first time using a high-mounted camera to transform the room into a dancer-controlled instrument. (Link)

  • Gave a lecture-demo-workshop on my duet with Tuli (Link)

  • I got a new day job :)

  • Started Gushers and had 2 guests (Link)

Shoutout to collaborators and organizers Dan Galat, Kelly Quesada, Cacie Miller, Irene Hsiao, Adam Shead, Rin Peisert, Ben Willis, Josh Harlow, Tuli Bera, Amanda Marais, Cristal Sabbagh, Kate Milleker, Tomeka Reid, olula negre, Gaby Martinez, Helen Lee, Chris Knowlton, Naydja Bruton, Haruhi Kobayashi, Paul Giallorenzo, Bill Harris, Stephan Moore, Sharon Udoh, and Nick Turner.

I'm looking forward to some new projects in 2026 that will continue to lead me outside my comfort zone. Onward.
